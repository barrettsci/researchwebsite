Luke T Barrett


About

I do research to drive more sustainable use of the coastal marine environment. Im currently a postdoctoral research fellow splitting my time between the SALTT lab at the University of Melbourne (Australia) and the Animal Welfare research group at the Institute of Marine Research (Norway). Ive also recently been a visiting researcher at The Nature Conservancy.

Theme 1: Ecologically beneficial aquaculture
Aquaculture modifies coastal marine environments by providing structure and food and altering nutrient dynamics. A better understanding of these effects can help achieve more positive outcomes, mitigate negative impacts, and provide a net enhancement of ecosystem services for coastal communities.
Recent research: Barrett et al.2022

Theme 2: Fish health in salmon farming
Intensive salmon farming has brings economic benefits, but also problems. Salmon farms amplify pathogens and parasites, especially sea lice. Severe louse outbreaks cause welfare issues in farmed salmon and threaten their relatives in wild (we estimate that around 99% of salmon lice in Norway originate from salmon farms). New methods are needed to prevent, monitor and treat infestations without compromising animal welfare standards.
Recent research: Barrett et al.2022

Theme 3: Rocky reef ecology, conservation and restoration
Seaweed and kelp habitats are diverse and productive, but have come under threat from multiple stressors including eutrophication, sedimentation and overgrazing. Im interested in the conservation of seaweed habitats in degraded coastal areas, including the role of invasive macroalgae as surrogate habitat and strategies to manage overabundant sea urchins and allow recovery of kelp ecosystems.
Recent research: Barrett et al.2019
